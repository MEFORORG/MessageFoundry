**Inference:** The 70.68% pass is an artifact of the idle-diluted prereg denominator, not evidence that query CPU explains the wall.

Evidence (Lens 1 + Lens 4, both high-confidence, mutually corroborating):
- The numerator is a windowed CPU **delta** over [snap0..snap24]; the denominator is a simple **arithmetic mean of instantaneous gauges** deflated by idle-ramp samples (snap0=0%, snap1=37%) that the delta numerator never credits with CPU. The pairing is inconsistent.

- Mechanism-inverted pass: the plateau intervals (box 92–94%, the actual wall) never exceed a 69.1% ratio. The gate is carried entirely by the 4 **collapse-tail** intervals (snaps 20→24) where box CPU **drops off the wall** to 82–88% while claim-storm worker-time spikes to ~82% (per-interval ratios 92–101%). QUERY-EXPLAINS-WALL "passes" precisely where the box is no longer at the wall.
- The eviction/truncation hedge that could have *rescued* the verdict is closed: the more-complete server-side aggregate still yields 64.6%.
- The 84.4% "box wall" denominator directly contradicts C3's verified prior (store at 92–93% at N=16) — a free consistency check the prereg's INC-16 flagged as skipped.

**What survives:** the *number* 70.68% survives on narrow contractual grounds (the prereg pinned "mean store_proc_cpu%", and the analyzer mechanically reads 70.68). **The robustness claim "query explains the wall" does not survive.** Because a known instrument defect (min=0 idle contamination the prereg did not intend) is what carries the pass, this is INCONCLUSIVE-INSTRUMENT, not a clean pass.

**Consequence:** N=16 was the ONLY arm to clear the blocking pre-gate. If it flips to INCONCLUSIVE, **no arm passes reconciliation → family precedence is not authoritative at any N → CONFIRMED is unreachable.** This alone forces WITHHELD.

**What would falsify:** a flat or scan-fully-tracked cpu_us_per_exec across arms — not observed; the rise is real. But this is a **necessary target condition only, not sufficiency**, and its authority is gated by the reconciliation that now fails.
