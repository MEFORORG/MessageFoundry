## 3. R and the decision (§3.1 / §3.3)
- **R ∈ [2, 3)** — highest per-shard rate meeting the PASS bar is **2/shard (C5-a)**; **3/shard fails,
  reproduced** (C5-b + c5-b2). **R < 3.62.**
- **Marginality annotation (§3.1):** at the sustaining boundary (2/shard) the backlog slope is **+1.9
  (comfortable, flatter than C3's c3-8 +4.04)**; 3/shard is not marginal — it is a hard collapse
  (slope +108/+111, ~50% delivered, ~85k stranded, growing `in_pipeline`).
- **§3.2 carve-out — does NOT fire (not deferred):** at the 3/shard collapse the **engine (max_core
  ~38%) and load-gen (<8%) are both clearly cool** — reproduced in both runs, both far below the ~85%
  bar, and the co-limited seam is nowhere near. Per §3.2, a fail with **neither box saturated → a
  legitimate design verdict, read the table straight.** (Store CPU ran 62→81% — elevated, but this is
  **not** claimed as "the store CPU was the wall": 80% is below C3-16's 92-93% saturation, and **naming
  the N=8 wall is C6's job, not C5's** — see §6.) The engine upsize did what it was meant to: it removed
  the prior 8-vCPU engine constraint, so the limit is in the code+store path **as it ships** (not the
  bench rig) and the answer is **decisive, not deferred**. Consistent with the "scale the engine tier"
  finding, not a contradiction.
- **Verdict:** **N-SIZING INSUFFICIENT** (independent of any claim rewrite; C4 = WITHHELD). R is raw
  latch-free capability; the D4-publishable rate is `N × R × 0.5`, i.e. even further below target.

## 6. On the N=8 wall — C5 does NOT name it  (STRUCK & CORRECTED 2026-07-12 per coordinator review §2)
**Retraction.** An earlier draft of this section read the collapse as *"a serialization/write-path
signature (`SOS_SCHEDULER_YIELD` + `LOGMGR_QUEUE`/`CHECKPOINT_QUEUE`) rather than raw CPU exhaustion."*
**That is struck — it was backwards, and it is the exact trap the C6 instrument exists to prevent.**

- `LOGMGR_QUEUE` and `CHECKPOINT_QUEUE` are **idle background waits** (log-writer / checkpoint threads
  *waiting for work*) — both on the standard benign-exclusion set. The magnitudes confirm it:
  `CHECKPOINT_QUEUE` (792,684 ms) sits in a cluster of background waits each ≈ **800,000 ms** over the
  800 s window (`DIRTY_PAGE_POLL`, `LAZYWRITER_SLEEP`, `XE_TIMER_EVENT`, `SQLTRACE_INCREMENTAL_FLUSH_SLEEP`,
  …) — threads asleep the *entire* run; `LOGMGR_QUEUE` (1.58M ms ≈ 2× window) = two log-writer threads
  asleep. `SOS_WORK_DISPATCHER` topped the raw list at 74-92M ms (pure SQLOS idle). **The dump was a raw
  top-N by `wait_time_ms`, never filtered through the exclusion set — the "rank-1 by default" trap.**
- **The wait that would prove a write-path wall is ABSENT:** `WRITELOG`, `PAGEIOLATCH_*`, `LCK_*`,
  `RESOURCE_SEMAPHORE*`, `PAGELATCH_*` — none appear in either collapse arm.
- **The one real, reproduced signal points the opposite way:** `SOS_SCHEDULER_YIELD` (~826k / 834k tasks)
  — quantum exhaustion, a **CPU-pressure** signal — alongside store CPU 62→81%.

**What stands: C5 does NOT name the N=8 wall.** Naming a wall requires a *convoy* (the C6 §3(a)
instrument), never a wait's rank. C5 measures `R` — that is all it measures. Any store-side lever
(including Phase-4 group-commit) must be justified independently; C5 provides **no** support for it, and
citing C5 in its favour would be the adjacency inference that walked back C2 and C4.
